## What "Special VPS Deals" Actually Means at BandwagonHost

BandwagonHost (operated by IT7 Networks) runs two parallel product worlds. The first is the regular catalogue — the Basic KVM, E-Commerce CN2 GIA-E, Singapore, Osaka, Tokyo, Hong Kong, Dubai, and SLA lines that are almost always in stock and can be ordered at any time. The second is the limited-edition world: small-batch plans with memorable names like THE PLAN, MINICHICKEN, BiggerBox Pro, MegaBox Pro, The Tokyo Plan, The Amsterdam Plan, The DC6 Plan, and The DC9 Plan.

The special deals are the second group. They share a few characteristics that make them both attractive and annoying:

- **Annual-only pricing** that is often one-third to one-fifth of the closest regular plan with similar specs.
- **Specific data centers and routes** baked in — you cannot freely migrate a MINICHICKEN plan the way you can shuffle a regular KVM between nine data centers.
- **Small batch releases and periodic restocks**, which is why stock monitoring sites like stock.bwg.net and HostMonit exist.
- **CPU fair-use limits** that are tighter than regular plans (for example, MINICHICKEN is capped at 25% of one core, BIGGERBOX at 25%, THE PLAN at 45%).

In other words, a special deal is a trade: you give up flexibility and guaranteed availability in exchange for a much lower price or a route configuration that BandwagonHost does not normally sell at that price point. If you treat them as opportunistic pickups rather than your only server, they make sense. If you build your whole project around one and it never restocks, you will be stuck.
